# Competitor Research

## Direct Competitors

### Adobe Color (color.adobe.com)
- Web-based color palette tool
- Camera capture on mobile app
- Full color theory tools (complementary, triadic, etc.)
- Deep Adobe ecosystem integration
- **Gap:** Complex, ecosystem-locked, not a fast mobile capture tool

### Coolors (coolors.co)
- Palette generation and exploration
- Camera capture feature on mobile app
- Share/export palettes
- Large community palette library
- **Gap:** Focused on generation, not identification. No Pantone, no pixel-precise capture.

### ColorSnap (Sherwin-Williams)
- Camera color matching to paint swatches
- Strong mobile experience
- **Gap:** Paint-brand locked, not for designers or general use

### Color Identifier apps (iOS/Android native)
- Many exist on app stores (Color Grab, ColorSnap, Palette Cam)
- Native camera access = better performance
- **Gap:** Not web-based, not shareable URLs, siloed

### Canva Color Palette Generator
- Upload image → extract palette
- **Gap:** Upload only, no live camera, no Pantone

## What They All Miss
- **Instant, precise live camera capture** with a clean mobile web UX
- **Shareable color cards** (a single captured color as a link/image)
- **Wit and personality** — all competitors are utilitarian
- **Speed** — most require multiple taps before you see a color name
